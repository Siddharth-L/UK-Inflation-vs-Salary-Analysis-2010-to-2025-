# UK Inflation vs Salary Analysis (2010 to 2025)

## Overview
A data analysis project exploring how UK wages across different sectors have kept up with inflation over the last 15 years, with a focus on the 2022 cost of living crisis.

## Key Questions Answered
- Which sectors have beaten inflation over the last 15 years?
- How did the 2022 cost of living crisis affect real wages?
- What is the cumulative purchasing power of workers in each sector?
- Which sector has the best real wage growth track record?

## Key Findings
- **Technology** is the only sector to consistently beat inflation, ending 2025 with purchasing power approximately 17% above 2010 levels
- **Finance** also outperformed, ending approximately 12% above baseline
- **Healthcare and Retail** workers have seen purchasing power eroded over time
- The **2022 cost of living crisis** (CPI peaked at 11.1%) wiped out years of real wage gains for most sectors in a single year
- **2023 to 2024** saw strong nominal wage growth as employers responded to the crisis, but for many sectors this was too late to fully recover lost ground

- **Inflation:** ONS Consumer Price Index (CPI), series D7G7
- **Wages:** ONS Annual Survey of Hours and Earnings (ASHE), median earnings by sector
